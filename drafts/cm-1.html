Introduction to Continuum Mechanics

Published: Wed 08 July 2015
By Saiwal Krishna

In Engineering.

tags: mechanics

Continuum Mechanics deals with treating bodies as a continuous media instead of lumped mass as in classical mechanics. In this series of posts, I have tried to give a better understanding of the mathematical approach we take in describing the motion of a solid deformable body.

Solid Body

In the study of solid bodies, we usually consider them as a point object with the total mass at the center of mass and apply the equations of motion to the body w.r.t the center of mass. This approach is acceptable when we want to study the motion od discreet bodies which do not undergo deformation on their own. But when we start to talk about the forces experienced by each point of mass in the body we can no longer work with the concept of lumped mass. A solid body would have to be lumped into infinitely many point masses to get a reasonably accurate approximation. This is where the field of continuum mechanics comes into picture.

We start by defining a set of mathematical rules which govern the motion of the body. Each point in the body is defined by a set of co-ordinates and the motion is described by a mathematical equation.
