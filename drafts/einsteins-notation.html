    The need for index notation

    The index/indicial notation was deviced by Einstein while he was working on his theory of relativity and he needed a concise way of writing down his equations which often ran into multiple lines. This also helped him summarise a set of similar equations in one generalized equation. We will take a look at how this is done starting from the basics.

    How does it work?

    Lets say we have to represent the following three equations of motion in the three directions 1, 2, 3 corresponding to x,y and z directions respectively into one.

    $$ v_1 = u_1 + a_1t $$ $$ v_2 = u_2 + a_2t $$ $$ v_3 = u_3 + a_3*t $$

    we write all the above equations as:

    $$ v_i = u_i + a_i*t

    where the index i takes up values of 1, 2 and 3 giving us three equations.
